A day in the life of

A ScraperWiki
Data Scientist

My name’s Thomas Levine, and I’m a Data Scientist at ScraperWiki.

To me, data science is a combination of software engineering and statistics. You need to be flexible, inquisitive and rigorous.

I like getting almost forensic with data. Recently we were scraping some PDFs and I could tell, by looking at the files, that they were edited by hand in Microsoft Word, then converted to PDF, and that somewhere along the way, they were sometimes printed out and scanned. Knowing that, we were able to correct the data better than our customer ever imagined, because we knew it almost better than they did.

Our Process

We pride ourselves on following an agile, transparent process for satisfying our customers’s needs. It includes:

  1. Investigation. We collaboratively create a brief, and scope the data project.
  2. Getting data. Whether it’s internal or external, PDFs or Excel spreadsheets, web pages or other databases.
  3. Cleaning data. Turning postcodes into points on a map, or structuring and matching fuzzy data, like company names and time formats.
  4. Analysing data. Whether it’s crafting custom maps and visualisations, or preparing the data for export into Tableau or your business intelligence software.
  5. Support (SLA). Data changes, and ScraperWiki’s here for the long haul â€“ we ensure your data is always available and always up to date.

Case Study

Agricultural data science for media blue-chip, Agra Informa

When B2B data publisher Agra Informa needed to move 10 years’ worth of commodity pricing data (buried deep inside hundreds of PDFs) into a new digital platform, they came to ScraperWiki.

Our team of data scientists fed documents from Informa’s FTP server into ScraperWiki’s exclusive PDF extractor, before cleaning and structuring the data, and piping it into a Tableau visualisation on Informa’s publishing platform.

“ScraperWiki did just what we asked them to do. Delivery of finished product was very good and responsiveness to customer queries was excellent.”

With the new, clean data, Agra Informa could offer a better and more accurate service to its digital subscribers – increasing revenue and reducing print and distribution costs. ScraperWiki’s platform ensures the data is always fresh and always accountable.
