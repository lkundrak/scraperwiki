What is ScraperWiki?

There’s lots of useful data on the internet – crime statistics, government spending, missing kittens…

But getting at it isn’t always easy. There's a table here, a report there, a few web pages, PDFs, spreadsheets… And it can be scattered over thousands of different places on the web, making it hard to see the whole picture and the story behind it. It’s like trying to build something from Lego when someone has hidden the bricks all over town and you have to find them before you can start building!

To get at data, programmers write bits of code called ‘screen scrapers’, which extract the useful bits so they can be reused in other apps, or rummaged through by journalists and researchers. But these bits of code tend to break, get thrown away or forgotten once they have been used, and so the data is lost again. Which is bad.

ScraperWiki is an online tool to make that process simpler and more collaborative. Anyone can write a screen scraper using the online editor. In the free version, the code and data are shared with the world. Because it's a wiki, other programmers can contribute to and improve the code.

If you're not a programmer, or if you want to tackle a large or complex task, you can ask the ScraperWiki team to organise it for you.

Company history and people

ScraperWiki was started by Julian Todd and Aidan McGuire. The company has seed funding from 4iP, and the founders. We're based in Liverpool (engineering) and London (sales), UK.

Francis Irving (CEO, @frabcus) was founding developer at democracy startup mySociety. He founded developer tool TortoiseCVS, which with its successors is used by tens of millions of people. Aine McGuire (CMO, @ainemcguire) previously founded Pygmalion, the first UK Microsoft Gold Certified Partner company, which has a major focus on education and partner skills. Julian Todd (CTO, @goatchurch) created TheyWorkForYou.com, being first to scrape the UK Parliament back in 2003, inspiring the Sunlight Foundation and others. He dives and caves, but would never cave dive. Aidan McGuire (CFO, @amcguire62) founded three software businesses under the Blue Fountain brand — Japanese spreadsheets, supply chain management and content management. He has a banking background.
Ross Jones (VP of Engineering, @rossjones) has been described as "the best coder I had ever seen, simple as that". This is the 4th startup that he's worked for. He's one of the founders of the DoES Liverpool makerspace. Nicola Hughes (Data Journalism Advocate, @DataMinerUK) was a digital media producer at CNN. She sifted social media data to get the news before the wires, enabling CNN to be the first to report from the ground on the Abuja bombing. She owns a pet axolotl. Zarino Zappia (Designer, @zarino) researches creative participation in open data at Oxford University. Kinga Szczesniak (Sales Support Manager, @kinga82) has a background in public administration and spent some of her training at The Chancellery of the President of the Republic of Poland. Judith Townend (Community events, @jtownend) is a journalist and researcher, specialising in media law and ethics. She helped co-ordinate ScraperWiki's Hacks and Hackers tour across the UK and Ireland in 2010/11.

Thanks also to: Richard Pope, product manager for the first version of ScraperWiki. Tom Mortimer-Jones, Anna Powell-Smith, Mike Richardson, and Sym Roe who helped to build it. Mark James for the Silk Icons. Tom Steinberg and Jamie Arnold for mentoring. Francis Davey for legal advice. We also offer bespoke igloos.

Since you were probably wondering, the ScraperWiki logo is a "wheel tractor-scraper". It’s like a huge cheese slice for moving soil. The logo itself was designed by James Weiner.
