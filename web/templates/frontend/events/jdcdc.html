Why we need you Click to find out more

There are mountains of documents and websites out there that contain essential data about the US, but someone needs to liberate and analyze it to keep businesses and government accountable.

ScraperWiki, together with American University School of Communication, The Washington Post, and the Knight Foundation are doing just that. We need journalists, students, computer scientists, programmers, statisticians and public officials to dig up the data!

All you need is enthusiasm and a laptop – we’ll be supplying the food, drink and prizes thanks to support from the Sunlight Foundation, J-Lab and Associated Press. Entry is free but space is limited. So check out the three streams below, and reserve a ticket before they go.

Pick a stream Click to see the streams

Stream A: Journalism Data Derby

Use data to uncover facts and drive your story home. Work in teams to identify stories, create hypotheses, and then gather, clean, analyse and visualise the data you need. The most innovative approaches and original ideas will win prizes.

This stream is perfect for journalists with or without programming experience, or anyone who wants to dive deep into the production of a data story.

Stream B: Liberate the Data

Tell us what data
you’d like liberated!

We want to open up more data, and you’re the people to do it! This stream is the place for experienced Python, PHP or Ruby coders. Come pick up some high-level scraping skills and then get in the driver’s seat by liberating data for the Data Derby.

We’ll have a list of inaccessible datasets on hand that we need your help to access. And, of course, the most productive liberators will be recognized with prizes!

Stream C: Learn to Scrape

If you already have some basic Python or Ruby skills, but want to know more about using ScraperWiki to get at more of the data you want, this is the stream for you. We’ll be holding two tutorial sessions: one in Python on Friday morning, and one in Ruby on Friday afternoon.

By the end of Friday you’ll have all the knowledge you need to come back on Saturday and join a race team in Stream A or liberate some data in Stream B.
